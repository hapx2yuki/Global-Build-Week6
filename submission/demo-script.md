# Demo Video Script

Review render duration: 2:37.845. Hard limit: under 3:00. English voice, burned-in English captions, and a matching `.srt` sidecar.

The review film is rendered from the versioned Remotion source in `video/criteriaforge/`. Every product screen comes from the public FounderBrief replay or the separate live local self-test. Local captures and narration are intentionally excluded from the repository. Replace the temporary macOS synthetic voice with the creator's recording, or another narration source with verified public-use terms, before the public YouTube upload.

## 0:00–0:13 — The authority problem

**Visual:** Three staggered evaluation cards enter on a dark inspection surface. The cards remain deliberately plausible until the final result is stamped `BLOCKED`.

**Voice:** “Codex can build faster than many product owners can inspect. But when an AI silently drops an explicit non-goal, or invents a business decision, asking another AI for a score does not restore the owner’s intent. The rules can move with the answer.”

## 0:13–0:32 — Change the authority model

**Visual:** The real source-intake screen moves into focus. Founder memo, working-session record, and Git snapshot appear as private, hashed source cards. A per-run send-approval boundary pulses separately.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge changes the authority model. Original documents, spreadsheets, images, video metadata, and Git evidence stay on the user’s Mac. GPT-5.6 may propose an eight-section Product Constitution, but the source language remains authoritative, and material uncertainty returns to the human as a question.”

## 0:32–0:52 — Human ratification and five compile gates

**Visual:** The eight fixed sections appear beside the real Constitution screen. A blue `AI proposal` moves toward a green `Human ratified` state. The film then shows the real compile screen while all five safeguards pass one by one.

**Voice:** “The owner approves, rejects, or directly edits each consequential rule. Before version one point zero can exist, five safeguards must pass: intent completeness, human ratification, evaluability, consistency, and three-run stability. There is no average, no automatic approval, and compiled versions are immutable.”

## 0:52–1:11 — Three independent evaluations

**Visual:** Three independent run rails advance in parallel and converge on the same gap. The real `Intent–Observed–Evidence–Gap` screen remains readable beside them.

**Voice:** “Now the fixed artifact is evaluated three times against that exact Constitution. This public experience is a replay of three real GPT-5.6 Sol evaluations, not a live call. Results are not compressed into one score. Each finding shows intent, observed reality, locally verified evidence, and the precise gap.”

## 1:11–1:27 — Exact FounderBrief findings

**Visual:** A drawn locator line connects the real finding to a citation-verification card showing source ID, source location, and SHA-256 status.

**Voice:** “The recorded FounderBrief case converged on the same hard failures: an explicit exclusion of investor material disappeared, and team accounts plus recurring billing were invented without asking. Every adopted citation passed the local source identifier, location, and SHA-256 checks.”

## 1:27–1:48 — Bounded Codex repair

**Visual:** The Product Constitution remains locked while only the gap moves into a detached Git worktree. Allowed files, approved command arrays, tests, and the full patch are verified before the recorded repair screen changes to `VERIFIED`.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge gives Codex the gap, not authority over the Constitution. A human ratifies exact files, forbidden paths, command arrays, acceptance conditions, and time limits. Codex runs in a detached worktree. The app verifies the read-only Constitution hash, changed files, tests, and full patch before human application.”

## 1:48–2:10 — Same-condition re-evaluation and honest stopping

**Visual:** The recorded before/after screen identifies the same contract hash. It then changes to the separate live local `stability insufficient` result and visibly disables repair.

**Voice:** “The repair is evaluated again only under the same Constitution, model, reasoning effort, prompt version, and Schema version. A separate live local self-test drafted a Constitution, calibrated it three times, froze three hundred fourteen Git evidence segments, and evaluated three times. Those runs disagreed, so CriteriaForge blocked repair instead of voting the disagreement away.”

## 2:10–2:27 — Technical proof

**Visual:** Six implementation cards identify ChatGPT OAuth, strict JSON Schema, private SQLite, content hashes, loopback-only serving, and the physical public-build split. The real public diagnostics screen and verified test counts remain visible.

**Voice:** “Under the hood, CriteriaForge reuses ChatGPT OAuth through the Codex CLI, validates strict JSON Schemas, stores private state in SQLite and content-addressed files, and serves the local app only on a random loopback port. The Vercel build physically excludes every local API route.”

## 2:27–2:38 — Close

**Visual:** The product promise resolves into three lines: human intent becomes executable, disagreement becomes visible, and authority stays human. Public demo and repository addresses remain on screen.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge makes human judgment reusable without pretending AI is perfectly deterministic. Human intent becomes executable. Disagreement becomes visible. And authority stays human.”
