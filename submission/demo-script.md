# Demo Video Script

Target: 2:45. Hard limit: under 3:00. English voice and accurate English captions.

## 0:00–0:15 — The authority problem

**Screen:** Two plausible but inconsistent judgments of one FounderBrief artifact.

**Voice:** “Codex can build faster than a product owner can inspect. But when the AI silently drops a non-goal or invents a business decision, another AI score does not restore the founder’s intent.”

## 0:15–0:35 — The promise

**Screen:** Founder memo, working-session record, Git snapshot in the source register.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge turns original evidence into a human-ratified, executable Product Constitution. Codex may apply it, but may never silently redefine it.”

Point out that originals are private and the public case is fictional.

## 0:35–1:05 — Compile judgment

**Screen:** Eight-section Constitution and one highlighted AI proposal.

**Voice:** “GPT‑5.6 proposes eight fixed sections, but a material ambiguity returns to the human. I approve, reject, or edit it in the source language.”

Approve the one rule. Open the compile review and show all five gates.

**Voice:** “Intent, ratification, evaluability, consistency, and three-run stability must all pass. There is no average and no automatic approval.”

## 1:05–1:40 — Evaluate with evidence

**Screen:** Recorded-run banner, formal evaluation, FR-01 and FR-02.

**Voice:** “This is a replay of three real `gpt-5.6-sol` evaluations—not a live call. The same Constitution, target, model settings, input hash, and schema were used three times.”

Open one finding and jump to the cited source location.

**Voice:** “All three runs found the same two hard gaps: the investor-material non-goal was dropped, and team accounts plus recurring billing were invented without a question. Every adopted citation passed a local locator and SHA‑256 check.”

## 1:40–2:15 — Repair without transferring authority

**Screen:** Remediation brief, exact allowed files, forbidden paths, commands, worktree steps.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge gives Codex the gap, not authority over the Constitution. The repair runs in a detached worktree. The app verifies the read-only Constitution hash, changed files, approved tests, and patch before a human can apply it.”

Replay the repair, then show the before/after screen with “Same contract hash.”

## 2:15–2:35 — Technical proof

**Screen:** Very brief terminal or repository evidence.

**Voice:** “The implementation uses ChatGPT OAuth through the Codex CLI, strict JSON Schemas, private SQLite storage, content-addressed evidence, three-run aggregation, and a public build that physically excludes every local API route.”

Show the passing test count and public-build separation message. Do not dwell on terminal text.

## 2:35–2:45 — Close

**Screen:** Product promise and project URL.

**Voice:** “CriteriaForge makes human judgment reusable without pretending AI is perfectly deterministic. Human intent becomes executable; disagreement becomes visible; authority stays human.”

## Recording checks

- [ ] Public replay and actual local execution are verbally distinguished.
- [ ] No personal account name, notification, browser history, private path, or token is visible.
- [ ] Text is readable at 1080p.
- [ ] Audio and captions explicitly name Codex and GPT‑5.6.
- [ ] Exported duration is under 180 seconds.
- [ ] YouTube visibility is Public.
- [ ] Playback and every linked URL work while logged out.
